That Power is Given us to Become the Sons of God,

January 6

But as many as received him, to them gave he power to become the sons of God, even to them that believe on his name. John 1:12.

Divine sonship is not something that we gain of ourselves. Only

 

to those who receive Christ as their Saviour is given the power to

 

become sons and daughters of God. The sinner cannot, by any power

 

of his own, rid himself of sin. For the accomplishment of this result,

 

he must look to a higher Power. John exclaimed, “Behold the Lamb

 

of God, which taketh away the sin of the world.” Christ alone has

 

power to cleanse the heart. He who is seeking for forgiveness and

 

acceptance can say only,—

 

“Nothing in my hand I bring;

 

Simply to Thy cross I cling.” But the promise of sonship is made

 

to all who “believe on his name.” Every one who comes to Jesus in

 

faith will receive pardon.19

 

The religion of Christ transforms the heart. It makes the worldly-

 

minded man heavenly-minded. Under its influence the selfish man

 

becomes unselfish, because this is the character of Christ. The dis-

 

honest, scheming man becomes upright, so that it is second nature to

 

him to do to others as he would have others do to him. The profligate

 

is changed from impurity to purity. He forms correct habits; for the

 

gospel of Christ has become to him a savor of life unto life.20

 

God was to be manifest in Christ, “reconciling the world unto

 

himself.” Man had become so degraded by sin that it was impossible

 

for him, in himself, to come into harmony with Him whose nature

 

is purity and goodness. But Christ, after having redeemed man from

 

the condemnation of the law, could impart divine power, to unite with

 

human effort. Thus by repentance toward God and faith in Christ, the

 

fallen children of Adam might once more become “sons of God.”21
